Picto AI

Unleash your creativity effortlessly! Simply type in your prompt, and watch as our AI generates unique images for you, perfect for book covers, photos, illustrations, art, and more.

  • My role

    Research & product strategy, user testings, user flows, interaction, visual design, Front-end Prototype [HTML/CSS/JS]

  • Team members

    Co-founders
    Product designers
    Full-Stack Developers

  • Timeline

    2 Months

Problem Statement

  • The process of creating and posting images is not as fast and simple as desired.

  • No ability to directly publish the image from picto into the draft or existing article/ Change covers for books.

  • No entry point of picto inside the editor. Users had to manually download the generated image and then come back to the editor and upload that image. That's too time consuming and some writers might loose their focus.

Goals

  • Innovation and Creativity: Foster a culture of innovation within the team and ensure that Picto AI stands out as a truly avant-garde project, pushing the boundaries of both artificial intelligence and artistic expression.

  • User-Centric Design: Prioritize user experience by employing user-centric design principles. Aim to create an interface that is intuitive and engaging, making the fusion of AI and artistic brilliance accessible and enjoyable for users.

  • Simplify User Interface: Streamline the user interface to make it more intuitive and user-friendly, reducing the learning curve for new users.

  • Optimize Image Creation Workflow: Enhance the image creation process to make it faster and more efficient, allowing users to generate high-quality artwork with minimal effort.

  • Customization Options: ntroduce additional customization features, such as advanced editing tools and filters, to empower users to personalize their artwork according to their preferences.

Target audience

Users who are looking for a way to enhance their written work, stories, etc. using visually captivating images.

  • Authors and Novelists: Writers who work on long-form fiction or non-fiction projects.

  • Comic Writers: Creators specializing in comic book and graphic novel writing, leveraging Picto AI to enhance and visualize their narratives through dynamic and captivating visuals.

  • Storytellers and Scriptwriters: Individuals engaged in crafting narratives for various mediums, such as film, television, or digital media, interested in visualizing aspects of their storytelling.

  • Bloggers and Content Writers: Individuals who regularly produce written content for blogs, websites, or online platforms.

  • Online Platform Contributors: Writers contributing to online platforms, forums, and communities.

Research methods

To gather valuable insights and inform the development of Picto AI for writers and creators, various research methods were employed. Some research methods:

  • Interviews with Creators: Holding one-on-one interviews with writers, comic creators, and storytellers to delve deeper into their creative processes, pain points, and aspirations regarding visual elements.

  • Competitor Analysis: Analyzing existing tools and platforms catering to writers and creators to identify strengths, weaknesses, and gaps in the market that Picto AI can address.

  • Focus Groups: Organizing focus group discussions with representatives from the target audience to gather collective insights, opinions, and collaborative ideas.

  • Analytics and Usage Data: Utilizing our analytics tools within our existing platform to collect data on how users interact.

  • Usability Testing: Conducting usability testing sessions with representative users to evaluate the ease of use, effectiveness, and overall user experience of Picto AI. Observing how users navigate the platform, create visual content, and interact with features helps identify areas for improvement.

  • A/B Testing: Implementing A/B testing to compare different versions of Picto AI and determine which variations resonate better with users. This method involves presenting users with different interfaces or features and analyzing their preferences, click-through rates, and engagement metrics to inform design decisions.

When we created the wireframes for Picto AI, we focused on mapping out how users would navigate through the app, from creating and customizing images to changing cover photos. We included notes and questions along the way to encourage collaboration and address any user concerns.
