Debate and Discussions

A way for readers to share their opinions and debate on a particular article with the help of comments.

Our goal is to provide substantive commentary for a general readership. By screening submissions, we have created a space where readers can exchange intelligent and informed commentary that enhances the quality of our news and information.

  • Facilitate readers' discussion on Scroll and other products.

  • Introduce a friction-free account creation.

  • Create intuitive Moderation experience.

Pattern

  • Entry points; on share-bar and a button below the article that opens the Comments experience for readers.

  • On mobile, full-screen comments modal

  • 2 ways to solve registration before commenting; 1. a reader comments, enters their email id, hit a 'Post' button. Upon verifying their email, the comment shows up on Scroll. 2. a reader hits a login/share your thoughts button, gets redirected to login/register screen, then gets redirected back to article they were reading. In this scenario, they might have to hit the comment button again to write their thoughts.

  • Would there be avatars? What would they show? First letter of name, default image set, etc? If friction-less registration (with only email), we won't be able to show names.

  • Can readers share individual comments? If so, upon accessing the link, how does the comments experience open?

  • On desktop, would comments slide in?

  • A link to page with details about rules and civility guidelines for commenting. If this copy is short enough, maybe it can be part of the comments window itself.

  • Dig deep into how does threads, reporting/flagging comments, deleting comments, etc works

  • A streamlined design for a commenter that is logged out. Yellow notification design as base and maybe divide it into 'Step 1' 'Step 2' sort of visual design for ease of understanding.

  • If moderation mechanism exists, maybe send an email to the reader once their comment is approved.

Touchpoints

A step by step final design coverage -

Comments entry points - There are 2 entry points for the comments. One is at the bottom of the article and another one at the share sheet. For mobile the first entry point lies next to share meta to avoid scroll at the way down to comment on the article.

Comments Entry points on desktops and mobile. On mobile comments entry point is a part of sticky share sheet.

Comment Panel (logged-out) - Shows Comment Panel with Logged-out UX and Reply UX. Entry points.

Comment Panel (logged-in) - Shows Comment Panel with Logged-in UX, Pending UX and Reply UX.

Comment Posting - This shows the loading animation upon hitting CTA buttons in comment composer.

New Register + Login - Better account creation / login UX.

OTP Screen - OTP screen where a reader enters the OTP sent to email for verification of the account.

OTP Email Template - Email template with the verification OTP sent to reader.

OTP Resending - A way to tell the reader that the OTP is resending. Custom dots and text is used at the same place of the timer.

Comment share Modal - Modal window for sharing a comment.

Single comment view - When a Reader lands to comments panel via social media or a permalink and sees an individual comment which was shared in the comment panel. Reader can click on "Back to all comments" to see all the comments.

Single comment Threaded view - Threaded view for the single comment. When a reader lands via social media and sees a single comment and replies on that individual comment. Reader can click on "Back to all comments" to see all the comments.

Reader Banned - When the reader is banned for posting comments on scroll. Reply option is also removed for banned reader.

Comments Closed - Disables the Comment Composer and shows a message to the reader

Unverified Reader - A scenario where the email address is unverified. So in order to post a comment reader needs to verify his email address. The reason for this is : Any anonymous reader can post some S**t about something with someone’s identity. We don’t want to build that kind of bad environment for scroll readers. Therefore each and every comment is manually approved by a human.

Blank State - When the article is open for commenting but the debate is not started i.e. no comments.

Skeleton UI - Displayed when comments are being fetched. This screen comes during the loading time of comments from API.

Comments moderation FAQ's - Guidelines for discussions on Scroll. FAQ's for the reader to get more information about debate and discussions.

Error Scenarios - Some of the error indicators

Approved comment - Email - Email sent to the readers to notify that comment is approved and start a debate.
